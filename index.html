Audio Reproduction

Amplifier

Our parametric speaker is made from a piezoelectric transducer array connected to a custom class D amplifier. The audio signal is generated by a wide bandwidth audiophile DAC that lets us do all of our preprocessing digitally, before sending it out to the amplifier and array. As you can seen from the diagram on the right, we have constructed a simple AM transmitter in GNU Radio that lets us convert a signal at baseband, which for now is just a sin wave, into a signal modulated at the 40khz resonant frequency of the transducers.

Tracking Software

The base assembly

The second major component of the project is the tracking aspect. We are currently developing our own tracking platform that we will use along with our speaker array to deliver audio to our target. Instead of having the speaker placed on the table, which will limit the range it can reach, we plan to mount the structure on a wall or ceiling. The base is turned using a ring gear and a pinion gear; a raspberry pi camera will be attached to the transducer array and once the subject is selected, the transducer array will continuously follow the subject.
