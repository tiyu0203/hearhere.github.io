Underlying Physics

Conventional loudspeakers are fundamentally limited in their directivity due the nature of their construction. To better understand this property, we can approximate the pressure radiating from a loudpeaker by computing the far-field pressure radiating from a circular piston. As we can see from the figure, the directivity of the sound field is determined by the product of the wavenumber ka and the piston radius a. This means that for a given frequency, directivity is directly proportional to the size of the transducer.

Parametric arrays operate by using their transmission medium as a demodulator to produce low frequency sound waves from higher frequency ones. The compression and rarification of air caused by the ultrasonic waves results in formation of low frequency waves that can be controlled using a modulation scheme similar to AM transmission.
